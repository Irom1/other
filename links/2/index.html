Civilization: Greece

By: Mori

Government

Many city-states of ancient greece were democracies. In those city-states only citizens (adult men that were not slaves) were allowed to own land or and take part in politics and law. In some other city-states they had rich rulers that owned the land and had powerful armies.

Religion

People worshiped gods and built temples to honor them. These gods were created when people made them as myths to explain some of the mysteries of life. People believed these myths, so the started building what now is the remains of temples.

Economics

The economy of ancient Greece was defined largely by the region's dependence on imported goods. Greece had good sea access and trade routes. The also had some pirates. Some greeks were farmers, planting wheat, harvesting olives, and weeding their gardens. Women also would weave and spin cloth.

Art and Architecture

The greeks made lots of pottery and things that represented the them. Most art was public for display and government funded. Columns had many different artistic designs and were in a lot of greek architecture.

Science and Technology

The ancient greeks were curious about themselves and the world and made many important discoveries. The greeks discovered things like the fact that the earth floated on its own in space, turning on its axis. They also tried to predict when they would have eclipses. Greece did not have very advanced technology unless it was in buildings.

Environment

Ancient Greece had a Mediterranean climate. The summers were dry and hot (75 degrees on average in the summer). The sea had a cool temperature with a slight breeze.

Social and Cultural Values

Greek philosophers valued logic. Greeks valued dancing, storytelling, art and music. Birds, dogs, goats, tortoises, and mice were all popular pets. Religious gods were also valued.

Civilization: Rome

By: Daniel and adapted by Mori

Government

Political slogans were painted on walls. Government buildings were in what was called the ‘forum’. There might be a public board for people to put things on in the forum. Two pairs of people were elected yearly.

Religion

The forum also had all the religious buildings. Some people had shrines in their houses. The forum had more than one religious building.

Economics

Shops had pictures to help explain what they sold on their shop windows. Shops and houses were sometimes combined. If you wanted water directly in your home, you had to be rich and pay lots of money.

Art and Architecture

People only had small windows for light. It was hard to build two story houses so many buildings were one story. Houses and shops were packed together. A houses main room was used for important occasions and visitors.

Science and Technology

Romans used aqueducts. They had heated baths which used a hypocaust. The romans had bridges as well.

Environment

The region of campania (pompeii) had fertile land. The Mediterranean sea bordered the roman republic and empire. The Romans conquered Northern Africa, Southern Europe and South Western Asia.

Social and Cultural Values

When writing, romans used a piece of metal, a bone, or some ivory. A school would have been small. It would have had 30 children at most. Since there was no public school you had to pay, but the cost for education was low.

Civilization: Egypt

By: Kyle and adapted by Mori

Government

The pharaoh had the most power. They ruled over the country. You could not oppose the pharaoh without getting punished.

Religion

The people of egypt prayed and made offers to the gods in which they believed in. They believed the afterlife. The people of egypt attempted to keep the dead people’s souls alive after they died.

Economics

Rivers gave the people have a reasonably easy way to get to other civilizations and trade. This resulted in Egypt being a big factor in economics.

Art and Architecture

The kings wrote on the walls of their temples about life. They also wrote about what grand deeds they have done that contributed to their civilization. This was sometimes used to tell others after them what “wondrous and amazing” things that they have done.

Science and Technology

This civilization created several tools to help them with their art, farming and other activities. These helped them to make their work more efficient, make them look better and trade. They were also used to go into battle, attack and defend.

Environment

Where these people were, it would often get too hot. This is why a lot of Egyptian people shave their heads. A lot of water areas existed so this made easy ways to get to places where you could trade with other civilizations.

Social and Cultural Values

This is the social pyramid with the people with the most power at the top, and the least power at the bottom:

1. Pharaoh

2. Government Officials - Nobles, Priests

3. Soldiers

4. Scribes

5. Merchants

6. Artisans

7. Farmers

8. Slaves, Servants

Civilization: China

By: Mori, Kyle and Daniel

Government

Kings and emperors ruled China. There were 3 or more dynasties.

Religion

Oracle bones were read by priests to predict the future. Oracle bones were put in a fire where they would crack and leave marks. Oracle bones were turtle shells and cattle bones

Economics

The silk road connected china to distant countries. Part of the silk road was in europe. The silk road was one of the most effective trade routes.

Art and Architecture

Many people had straw covered roofs. There were also animal pens. There was a cemetery.

Science and Technology

A cooking pot was found. There were arrowheads. There were also fish hooks and pottery. The doctors used acupuncture to heal people.

Environment

One river was the Huang He or Yellow river. Western china’s mountains make travel hard. There are deserts in Western China. The silt makes the yellow river appear yellow.

Social and Cultural Values

Buddhism was brought from india. Many people looked to Daoism and confucianism. People looked to Daoism and confucianism to find out why they had to suffer so much, but didn’t find helpful answers.
